# Advertising & Marketing × Marketing Playbook

## Overview

Marketing in an advertising/marketing agency is a peculiar thing: it's the cobbler's children who have no shoes. Agencies spend all day marketing their clients but often neglect their own brand, positioning, and lead generation. When they do focus on agency marketing, it's often ad-hoc, under-resourced, and disconnected from business development.

The Marketing function in agencies typically spans brand and positioning, content marketing, social media presence, thought leadership, awards submissions, events, PR, and increasingly, lead generation and ABM for new business. At most agencies, this is a lean team (1-5 people) that punches above its weight — or a function that doesn't formally exist and is cobbled together from spare creative time.

What makes this combination unique: Agency marketing is meta-marketing — marketers marketing to other marketers. The audience is sophisticated and cynical. Generic content falls flat. The competition for attention is fierce. Yet agencies have unfair advantages: creative talent, production capabilities, and deep knowledge of what actually works in marketing. AI can help agencies turn that advantage into consistent output without diverting client-facing resources.

---

## Value Driver Prioritization

1. **Scale Impact Without Overhead** — HIGHEST RELEVANCE
   - Agency marketing teams are chronically under-resourced
   - The people who could create content are busy on client work
   - AI enables 1-2 people to produce what would otherwise require 5-10

2. **Replace or Radically Augment Headcount** — HIGH RELEVANCE
   - Content production is labor-intensive
   - Repurposing, distributing, and tracking content is repetitive
   - AI can automate content operations while humans focus on strategy and big ideas

3. **Consolidate Tech Stack with AI** — MEDIUM RELEVANCE
   - Marketing teams often use many tools: CMS, social scheduling, email, analytics
   - Integration gaps create manual work
   - One platform that connects content, distribution, and measurement

## Prioritized Use Cases

### 1. Content Production & Repurposing Pipeline
**Relevance**: High  
**Value Driver**: 1 (Replace/Augment Headcount)

**Pain Point**: 
Agency marketing knows they need content — case studies, blog posts, thought leadership, social content — but producing it consistently is nearly impossible. When talent is available, they're on client work. Content gets started but not finished, published but not repurposed, created but not measured.

**Solution**: 
monday.com content pipeline with AI-powered assistance. Plan content themes, assign and track production, use AI to draft and repurpose content, schedule across channels, and measure performance. Turn one piece of content into five formats automatically.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a Content Production Pipeline app for agency marketing teams.

Purpose: Plan, produce, repurpose, and track content across all channels with AI assistance.

Key features:
- Content calendar: Theme/topic, Content type (Blog/Case Study/Whitepaper/Social/Video/Podcast), Author, Due date, Status (Ideation/In Progress/Review/Approved/Published)
- Content briefs: Topic, Target audience, Key message, SEO keywords, Source material (projects, interviews, data)
- AI content assistance: Generate first drafts, suggest headlines, create social versions from long-form, generate email summaries
- Repurposing tracker: For each hero piece, track derivative content (Blog → LinkedIn post → Twitter thread → Email → Carousel)
- Channel distribution: Where will it publish? When? Track by channel
- Review workflow: Draft → Internal review → Legal (if needed) → Approved
- Performance tracking: Views, engagement, leads generated (by content piece)
- Dashboard: Content published this month, In progress, Pipeline by stage, Performance by content type, Top performing pieces

Include automations:
- When blog published, AI generates LinkedIn post and Twitter thread for review
- When content due date approaches, remind author
- When approved, schedule to publishing queue
- Monthly content performance report to Marketing Director
- Quarterly content audit: Flag underperforming content for refresh
```

**Outcome**: 
- 3x content output without additional headcount
- Consistent publishing cadence
- Every hero piece generates 3-5 derivative pieces
- Clear visibility into what's working

**Discovery Questions**:
- "How much content do you publish per month? How much would you like to publish?"
- "When you create a great piece of content, how many formats does it end up in?"
- "What's the bottleneck — ideas, time to produce, or something else?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
Agency content must be high-quality (the work represents capabilities). Case studies are gold but require client approval. Thought leadership positions the agency as experts. "Hero content" = substantial pieces; derivative = repurposed versions.

### 2. Case Study & Work Showcase Management
**Relevance**: High  
**Value Driver**: 3 (Scale Without Overhead)

**Pain Point**: 
Case studies are the currency of agency new business — proof that you've done great work. But producing them is painful: getting client approval, gathering assets, writing the story, updating the website. Most agencies have a fraction of the case studies they could have, and those they have are often outdated or missing key information.

**Solution**: 
monday.com case study pipeline that systematizes the process. Track every project that could become a case study, manage the approval and production workflow, store all assets in one place, and maintain an always-current library that BizDev and proposals can pull from.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a Case Study Management app for agency marketing and business development.

Purpose: Systematically capture, produce, and maintain client case studies for new business.

Key features:
- Case study candidate pipeline: Project name, Client, Work type, Results achieved, Case study potential (High/Medium/Low), Status (Candidate/Approval Requested/Approved/In Production/Published)
- Client approval workflow: Request sent, Contact, Approval status, Restrictions (can we name them? Use metrics? Show assets?)
- Production tracking: Writer assigned, Draft due, Review cycles, Publish date
- Asset management: Link to final creative assets, client logo, photos, video
- AI story assist: Generate first draft from project brief and results; suggest headlines and pull quotes
- Results database: Quantitative results searchable across all case studies (e.g., "show me all case studies with >50% engagement lift")
- Taxonomy: Industry, Service type, Platform, Target audience — for easy filtering
- Showcase status: Where is this published? (Website/Clutch/Awards submissions/Proposals)
- Dashboard: Case studies by status, By industry, By service, Results database, Approval pending, Recently published

Include automations:
- When project marked as case study candidate, notify Marketing and Account Director
- When client approval received, move to production and assign writer
- When published, update showcase status and notify BizDev
- Quarterly case study audit: Flag old case studies for refresh
- AI suggest case study candidates based on project results
```

**Outcome**: 
- 2-3x more case studies produced
- Always-current case study library
- Faster proposal assembly (pull relevant case studies instantly)
- Better new business win rate through stronger proof points

**Discovery Questions**:
- "How many case studies do you have? How many projects could be case studies?"
- "When you need a case study for a proposal, how quickly can you find the right one?"
- "What percentage of your projects have documented results?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
Client approval is often the biggest bottleneck. Some clients allow work showcase but not naming. Results (metrics) are essential but often not captured. Awards submissions use many of the same materials.

### 3. Awards Submission & Tracking
**Relevance**: Medium-High  
**Value Driver**: 3 (Scale Without Overhead)

**Pain Point**: 
Awards matter in advertising — they attract talent, impress clients, and justify premium pricing. But the awards process is chaotic: hundreds of shows, different deadlines, different formats, expensive entry fees. Agencies either undersubmit (missing opportunities) or oversubmit (wasting money and time on long shots).

**Solution**: 
monday.com awards management system. Track all relevant shows, plan submissions strategically, manage the production of entries, and track wins/losses for future planning. AI helps identify which work should be submitted where for best ROI.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build an Awards Submission Management app for agency marketing and creative leadership.

Purpose: Strategically plan, produce, and track awards submissions for maximum ROI.

Key features:
- Awards show database: Show name, Organizer, Categories, Entry deadline, Announcement date, Entry fees, Prestige level (Tier 1/2/3), Historical win rate
- Submission planning: Work to enter, Award show, Category, Entry fee, Strategic rationale, Status (Considering/Approved/Entry Started/Submitted/Result)
- Entry production: Assets needed, Case study board, Video requirements, Written submission, Deadline tracker
- AI entry assist: Draft written portions from case study materials; suggest optimal categories
- Budget tracking: Entry fees planned, Submitted, Won, ROI calculation
- Results tracking: Shortlisted, Won, Lost — with learnings
- Win celebration: Track wins for PR, website, social announcements
- Dashboard: Submissions this season, By status, Win rate, Spend vs wins, Work entered most, Shows with best ROI

Include automations:
- 60 days before major show deadline, alert Marketing and Creative Directors to plan entries
- When entry approved, create production checklist and assign owners
- When result announced, update status and notify team
- If won, trigger celebration workflow (PR, social, website update)
- Annual awards ROI report
```

**Outcome**: 
- More strategic awards spending (better ROI)
- Never miss a deadline
- More wins through better entry quality
- Wins are properly celebrated and leveraged

**Discovery Questions**:
- "How do you decide what to enter in awards shows? Is it strategic or ad hoc?"
- "What's your awards budget? What's your win rate?"
- "When you win an award, how do you leverage it?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
Tier 1 shows: Cannes Lions, D&AD, One Show, Effies, Clios. Entry fees can be $500-$1,500 per entry. "Case study board" = visual summary of work and results. Some shows require video submissions.

### 4. New Business Intelligence & Opportunity Tracking
**Relevance**: High  
**Value Driver**: 2 (Consolidate Tech Stack)

**Pain Point**: 
Agency new business is a chaotic mix of inbound inquiries, outbound prospecting, referrals, RFIs, and RFPs. Many agencies have no system — opportunities live in email, heads, and spreadsheets. Result: missed opportunities, no pipeline visibility, and no ability to forecast revenue.

**Solution**: 
monday.com CRM for agency new business. Track every opportunity from first touch to close (won or lost). Integrate with marketing to see which activities generate leads. AI identifies prospects matching your ideal client profile and helps prioritize where to focus.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a New Business Pipeline app for agency marketing and business development.

Purpose: Track opportunities from awareness to close, with full visibility into the new business pipeline.

Key features:
- Opportunity board: Prospect name, Industry, Size, Source (Inbound/Outbound/Referral/RFP), Service interest, Stage (Lead/Qualified/Meeting/Proposal/Negotiation/Won/Lost), Estimated value, Owner
- Contact management: Key contacts, Decision makers, Champions, Meeting history
- Activity logging: Calls, emails, meetings, proposals sent
- Proposal tracking: RFP/RFI requirements, Due date, Team assigned, Status
- AI lead scoring: Score prospects based on fit (industry, size, budget signals, engagement)
- Marketing attribution: Which content/event/campaign generated this lead?
- Win/loss analysis: When won or lost, capture reasons for learning
- Revenue forecasting: Weighted pipeline by stage
- Dashboard: Pipeline by stage, By source, Expected close this month/quarter, Win rate, Average deal size, Top opportunities

Include automations:
- When new lead comes in, score and assign based on criteria
- When stage changes, notify relevant people
- When proposal sent, set follow-up reminder
- When opportunity won/lost, prompt for win/loss analysis
- Monthly pipeline review report to leadership
```

**Outcome**: 
- Full visibility into new business pipeline
- Better lead prioritization through scoring
- Improved win rate through better follow-up
- Revenue forecasting for planning

**Discovery Questions**:
- "Do you know right now what's in your new business pipeline and when it might close?"
- "How do you prioritize which prospects to pursue?"
- "What percentage of RFPs do you win?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
Agency sales cycles are long (3-12 months for large clients). RFP process is formal with specific requirements. "Chemistry meeting" = agency/client compatibility assessment. AOR (Agency of Record) vs project relationships.

**Relevance**: Medium  
**Value Driver**: 1 (Replace/Augment Headcount)

**Pain Point**: 
Agencies use events for brand building and lead generation — hosting roundtables, sponsoring conferences, running webinars. But event logistics are complex, and measuring ROI is difficult. Most agency marketing teams are too stretched to do events well or consistently.

**Solution**: 
monday.com event management with full planning, promotion, execution, and follow-up tracking. AI helps with invitation copy, follow-up sequences, and post-event content. Measure leads generated and pipeline influenced by events.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build an Event Management app for agency marketing teams.

Purpose: Plan, promote, execute, and measure events and webinars for brand building and lead generation.

Key features:
- Event board: Event name, Type (Webinar/Roundtable/Conference/Hosted Event), Date, Goal (Brand/Lead Gen/Client Engagement), Budget, Owner, Status (Planning/Promoting/Live/Complete)
- Event planning: Venue/platform, Speakers, Topic, Target audience, Logistics checklist
- Promotion tracking: Invite list, Channels used, Send dates, RSVPs, Attendance
- AI promotion assist: Draft invitation emails, social posts, follow-up sequences
- Registration management: Registrant list with company/role info, Attendance tracking
```

**Outcome**: 
- More consistent event execution
- Higher registration-to-attendance rates

**Discovery Questions**:
- "How many events do you run per year? How do you measure their success?"
- "What happens to event attendees after the event? Do they become leads?"
- "How much time does event planning take your team?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
Agencies host events to showcase expertise (not just promote services). "Roundtables" bring prospects together for discussion. Webinars can be reused as content. Client events (maintaining relationships) vs prospect events (generating new business).

**Relevance**: Medium  
**Value Driver**: 1 (Replace/Augment Headcount)

**Pain Point**: 
Agencies know they should be active on social (especially LinkedIn) but struggle with consistency. Leadership wants to be "thought leaders" but doesn't have time to write. The agency account posts sporadically. Meanwhile, competitors are building audiences and winning mindshare.

**Solution**: 
monday.com social and thought leadership system. Plan themes and content, use AI to assist with drafting, manage approval workflows, schedule posts, and track engagement. Build personal brands for agency leaders alongside the agency brand.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a Social Media & Thought Leadership app for agency marketing teams.

Purpose: Manage social presence for the agency and its leaders, with AI-assisted content creation.

Key features:
- Content calendar: Post date, Platform (LinkedIn/Twitter/Instagram), Account (Agency/Individual), Topic, Content type (Original/Share/Repurpose), Status (Idea/Draft/Approved/Scheduled/Posted)

- AI content assist: Draft posts from bullet points, suggest variations, adapt long-form to social
- Approval workflow: Draft → Manager review → Leader approval (for personal accounts) → Scheduled
- Content library: Bank of approved posts, evergreen content, frequently used hashtags
- Engagement tracking: Likes, comments, shares, profile views, follower growth

- Dashboard: Posts this week, Engagement by platform, Follower growth, Top performing posts, Leader content by person

Include automations:
- When blog published, AI drafts social post for review
- When post approved, auto-schedule to platform
- When engagement on post exceeds threshold, notify team to respond
- Weekly social digest: What went out, how it performed, what's coming
- Monthly thought leadership report to leadership
```

**Outcome**: 
- Consistent social presence without full-time resource
- Leader thought leadership without leaders writing everything
- Growing audience and engagement
- Social supports content distribution

**Discovery Questions**:

- "Do your leaders post thought leadership content? Who creates it?"
- "What's your follower growth on LinkedIn?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
LinkedIn dominates B2B for agencies. Personal brands often outperform agency accounts. "Thought leadership" must be genuinely insightful (marketers are cynical). Employee advocacy amplifies reach.

## Industry Terminology Glossary

**AOR (Agency of Record)** — Primary agency relationship for a client, typically a longer-term engagement covering broad scope.

**RFP (Request for Proposal)** — Formal solicitation where clients request agencies to propose for business.

**RFI (Request for Information)** — Preliminary inquiry to gather information before an RFP; often used to narrow agency long lists.

**Chemistry Meeting** — Meeting where client and agency assess mutual compatibility (beyond capabilities).

**Credentials Deck / Creds** — Presentation showcasing agency capabilities, work, and team.

**ICP (Ideal Client Profile)** — Description of the ideal target client by industry, size, budget, etc.

**ABM (Account-Based Marketing)** — Targeting specific companies (not personas) with personalized marketing.

**Thought Leadership** — Content demonstrating expertise and perspective; positions individuals or agencies as industry authorities.

**Content Pillar** — Core themes around which content is organized (e.g., "creative effectiveness," "digital transformation").

**Case Study** — Documented client success story with situation, approach, and results.

## Typical Stakeholder Roles

**Primary Buyer: Marketing Director / CMO**
- Title: Marketing Director, Director of Brand & Communications, CMO (at larger agencies)
- Concerns: Resource constraints, proving marketing ROI, supporting new business, building agency brand
- Decision driver: "Can I do more marketing with my tiny team?"

**Technical Evaluator: Marketing Manager / Content Lead**
- Title: Marketing Manager, Content Manager, Social Media Manager
- Concerns: Ease of use, time savings, AI quality, channel integrations
- Decision driver: "Will this actually make my daily work easier?"

**Executive Sponsor: CEO / Managing Director**
- Title: CEO, Managing Director, President
- Concerns: Agency differentiation, new business pipeline, industry reputation
- Decision driver: "Does this help us win more business?"

**BizDev Stakeholder: Business Development Director**
- Title: Director of Business Development, New Business Director
- Concerns: Lead flow, proposal materials, case studies, pipeline visibility
- Decision driver: "Will this give me better ammunition and visibility?"

**End Users:**
- Marketing team, Content creators, Social media managers, Leadership (for thought leadership)

## Common Objections

**Objection**: "We're too busy with client work to focus on agency marketing."

**Response**: "That's exactly the problem we solve. monday.com with AI means your 1-2 person marketing team produces what would otherwise require 5. And it systematizes the work, so marketing happens consistently even when you're busy. What would change if you could 3x your content output?"

---

**Objection**: "We already have monday.com for projects. Do we need another workspace?"

**Response**: "Perfect — you already know the platform. Marketing can live in its own workspace or integrate with your existing setup. The point is: your marketing team gets AI assistance, content pipeline, case study management, and new business tracking — all connected to the projects and work that feeds it."

---

**Objection**: "AI content doesn't match our voice/quality."

**Response**: "AI doesn't replace your creative thinking — it accelerates it. Use AI to draft, then apply your expertise to refine. Turn a blank page problem into an editing problem. Agencies that figure out how to use AI effectively will produce more content and build bigger audiences. Agencies that don't will fall behind."
