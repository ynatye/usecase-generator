# Architecture, Engineering & Design × PR & Communications
## monday.com SE Playbook

### Industry Context

The Architecture, Engineering & Design (AEC) industry operates in a highly competitive, reputation-driven ecosystem where project visibility and thought leadership directly impact business development. AEC firms compete not just on technical capabilities, but on design innovation, sustainability leadership, and cultural relevance. Media coverage in trade publications like Architectural Record, Dezeen, ArchDaily, and ENR drives industry recognition, while design awards from AIA, ULI, and international competitions serve as critical differentiators in RFP responses and client acquisition.

PR & Communications teams in AEC firms face unique challenges managing multi-year project timelines, coordinating visual storytelling across project phases (schematic design through occupancy), and maintaining relationships with specialized design media that prioritize exclusive content and high-quality imagery. The visual nature of architecture requires sophisticated asset management, from early renderings to construction documentation to final photography, while the technical complexity demands communications professionals who can translate design concepts into compelling narratives for both trade and mainstream audiences.

The industry's project-based structure creates episodic PR opportunities—groundbreakings, topping out ceremonies, substantial completions, and occupancy milestones—each requiring coordinated campaigns across multiple stakeholders including clients, contractors, local officials, and media. Many mid-size firms struggle with resource allocation, often outsourcing to specialized AEC PR agencies while maintaining internal communications for business development and thought leadership initiatives.

### Department Profile
- **Typical Team Size:** 2-8 people (1-2 at boutique firms, 3-5 at mid-size firms, 5-8 at large international practices)
- **Key Stakeholders:** Design principals, project managers, business development, clients, contractors, design media, award program coordinators, photography/rendering vendors
- **Core KPIs:** Media placements per quarter, award submissions/wins ratio, speaking opportunities secured, social media engagement on completed projects, website traffic to project pages, RFP inclusion rate for award-winning projects
- **Common Tools Replaced:** Excel spreadsheets for media tracking, email chains for award submissions, Dropbox for asset management, manual calendar coordination for events, separate CRM for media contacts
