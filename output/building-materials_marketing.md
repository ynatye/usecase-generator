# Building Materials × Marketing
## monday.com SE Playbook

### Industry Context

The building materials industry operates in a complex ecosystem where marketing teams must navigate long sales cycles, technical specification processes, and multi-layered distribution channels. Marketing departments in this space face unique challenges including educating architects and specifiers on technical product attributes, managing relationships with distributors and dealers, and creating content that speaks to both technical decision-makers and end-users. The industry's reliance on building codes, CSI MasterFormat divisions, and submittal processes requires marketing teams to maintain detailed technical documentation while simultaneously driving brand awareness and demand generation.

Marketing in building materials is increasingly digital-first, with specification audiences researching products online before ever speaking to a sales rep. Teams must coordinate across multiple channels—from trade publications and industry events to BIM object libraries and specification software platforms. The rise of sustainable building practices and green certifications has added another layer of complexity, requiring marketing teams to track and communicate evolving environmental standards while managing product lifecycle communications from launch to discontinuation.

The department typically serves as the bridge between technical product management, sales teams, and external audiences including architects, engineers, contractors, distributors, and facility managers. Success requires deep coordination between brand marketing, product marketing, digital marketing, and channel marketing functions—often with limited headcount managing extensive product portfolios across multiple market segments.

### Department Profile
- **Typical Team Size:** 5-15 people (varying by company size and product portfolio breadth)
- **Key Stakeholders:** Product Management, Sales, Technical Services, Channel Partners, R&D, Regulatory Affairs
- **Core KPIs:** Lead generation, specification wins, brand awareness in architect/specifier community, trade show ROI, dealer engagement metrics, content performance, pipeline contribution
- **Common Tools Replaced:** Excel/Sheets for campaign tracking, Salesforce for marketing automation, disparate tools for asset management, email platforms, project management tools, event management systems
