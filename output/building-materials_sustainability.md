# Building Materials × Sustainability
## monday.com SE Playbook

### Industry Context

The building materials industry faces unprecedented pressure to decarbonize and demonstrate environmental responsibility throughout the entire product lifecycle. With construction accounting for nearly 40% of global carbon emissions, sustainability departments in companies producing cement, concrete, steel, lumber, insulation, and other building products are now strategic business units rather than compliance afterthoughts. These teams must navigate complex regulatory landscapes including the EU's Green Deal, California's Buy Clean Act, and emerging embodied carbon regulations while maintaining product performance and cost competitiveness.

Sustainability teams in this industry manage intricate workflows spanning cradle-to-gate lifecycle assessments, Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) development and maintenance, third-party verification processes, and multi-stakeholder collaboration with architects, contractors, and green building certification bodies like USGBC, GBCI, and BRE. The rapid evolution of standards—from LEED v4.1's embodied carbon pilot credits to upcoming mandatory whole-building LCA requirements—demands agile documentation and reporting systems that can adapt quickly while maintaining data integrity across hundreds of product variations and facility locations.

The department's success directly impacts market access, with major construction projects increasingly requiring verified sustainability credentials, carbon transparency, and circular economy metrics. Teams must coordinate between R&D for product innovations (like supplementary cementitious materials or bio-based alternatives), operations for facility-level emissions tracking, and commercial teams positioning products for green building markets—all while preparing for investor ESG reporting and Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) commitments.

### Department Profile
- **Typical Team Size:** 8-25 people (varies by company size and product portfolio breadth)
- **Key Stakeholders:** R&D, Operations, Quality, Commercial, Legal/Regulatory, External consultants, LCA practitioners, Certification bodies
- **Core KPIs:** EPD coverage %, embodied carbon reduction %, recycled content %, Scope 1/2/3 emissions reduction, LEED/BREEAM credit eligibility, regulatory compliance rate, time-to-market for sustainable products
- **Common Tools Replaced:** Excel trackers, SharePoint document libraries, email chains, OneNote project logs, GaBi/SimaPro LCA software disconnected from business systems
