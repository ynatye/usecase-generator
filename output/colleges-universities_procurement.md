## Industry Terminology

### Key Terms & Definitions

**Academic Procurement Terminology:**
- **HUB (Historically Underutilized Business)** - Minority/women-owned business certification requirements
- **Sole Source Justification** - Documentation required when only one vendor can provide needed goods/services
- **P-Card (Procurement Card)** - Corporate credit cards with built-in spending controls and approval workflows
- **Prevailing Wage** - Minimum wage rates required for construction projects using public funds
- **FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)** - Federal law protecting student education records
- **E-Rate** - Federal program providing telecommunications discounts to educational institutions
- **Cooperative Purchasing** - Group buying agreements among multiple institutions
- **Emergency Procurement** - Expedited purchasing procedures during declared emergencies
- **DBE (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise)** - Federal certification for minority/women/veteran-owned businesses

**Procurement Process Terms:**
- **RFP (Request for Proposal)** - Formal bidding process for complex purchases
- **ITB (Invitation to Bid)** - Sealed bid process for standardized goods/services
- **RFQ (Request for Quotation)** - Informal price quotation process for smaller purchases
- **IFB (Invitation for Bids)** - Competitive sealed bidding for construction projects
- **Best Value** - Evaluation method considering both price and qualitative factors
- **Lowest Responsible Bidder** - Selection criteria emphasizing vendor capability and price
- **Bid Protest** - Formal challenge to procurement award decisions
- **Change Order** - Modification to existing contracts affecting scope, price, or timeline
