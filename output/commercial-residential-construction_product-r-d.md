## Industry Terminology

**Building Information Modeling (BIM)**: Digital representation of physical and functional characteristics of buildings
**Design-Build**: Project delivery method where design and construction services are contracted by a single entity
**Prefabrication**: Construction process where building components are manufactured in a controlled factory environment
**Modular Construction**: Construction technique involving off-site manufacturing of standardized building sections
**Value Engineering**: Systematic method to improve project value by examining function versus cost
**Design Development (DD)**: Phase where design concepts are refined and detailed specifications developed
**Construction Documentation (CD)**: Detailed drawings and specifications used for construction and bidding
**Submittal**: Contractor-provided information about materials, equipment, or systems for approval
**Request for Information (RFI)**: Formal process for clarifying project documentation ambiguities
**Change Order**: Written modification to original contract scope, schedule, or cost
**Punch List**: List of incomplete or defective items identified during final inspection
**Commissioning**: Process of verifying and documenting that building systems perform as intended
**LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design)**: Green building certification program
**WELL Building Standard**: Performance-based system measuring building features impacting health
**International Building Code (IBC)**: Model building code developed for adoption by jurisdictions
**ASTM Standards**: Technical standards for materials, products, systems, and services
**Lifecycle Assessment (LCA)**: Methodology for evaluating environmental impacts throughout product lifecycle
