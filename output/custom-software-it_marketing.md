# Custom Software & IT Services × Marketing Playbook

## Overview

Marketing in custom software and IT services companies serves a specific purpose: generating demand for high-value, complex services sold through relationships. Unlike consumer marketing focused on broad awareness, services marketing must attract qualified prospects, demonstrate expertise, and nurture long consideration cycles. The audience is sophisticated buyers who see through hype.

The Marketing function typically spans brand and positioning, content marketing (thought leadership, case studies), demand generation, events, PR, and analyst relations. At services firms, marketing teams are typically small (2-15 people for a 500-person company) and must demonstrate clear ROI to justify investment.

What makes this combination unique: Services marketing is about credibility, not flash. The audience — CIOs, CTOs, and IT leaders — are highly skeptical and technically sophisticated. They research extensively before engaging. Content must demonstrate genuine expertise. The sales cycle is long, so marketing must nurture relationships over months. AI can help services firms produce expert content at scale without diluting quality.

## Value Driver Prioritization

1. **Scale Impact Without Overhead** — HIGHEST RELEVANCE
   - Marketing teams are lean relative to firm size
   - Content production is labor-intensive but essential
   - AI enables small teams to produce thought leadership at scale
   
2. **Replace or Radically Augment Headcount** — HIGH RELEVANCE
   - Content production, distribution, and reporting consume time
   - Marketing operations can be significantly automated
   - AI handles execution; humans focus on strategy

3. **Consolidate Tech Stack with AI** — MEDIUM RELEVANCE
   - Marketing uses many tools (CMS, email, social, analytics)
   - Integration gaps create manual work
   - One platform connecting content to leads improves efficiency

## Prioritized Use Cases

### 1. Thought Leadership Content Production
**Relevance**: High  
**Value Driver**: 1 (Replace/Augment Headcount)

**Pain Point**: 
Services firms differentiate through expertise, but producing thought leadership consistently is hard. Subject matter experts are busy on client work. Content gets started but not finished. When it is produced, it's often too technical or too promotional, missing the sweet spot.

**Solution**: 
monday.com content pipeline with AI assistance for thought leadership. Plan themes, collaborate with SMEs efficiently, use AI to accelerate drafting, and maintain a consistent publishing cadence.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a Thought Leadership Content Pipeline app for IT services marketing teams.

Purpose: Produce expert thought leadership content consistently with efficient SME collaboration.

Key features:
- Content calendar: Theme/topic, Content type (Blog/Whitepaper/Webinar/Podcast), Target audience, SME, Due date, Status
- Content brief: Topic, Key points, Target reader, SEO keywords, Source material (interviews, research, project learnings)
- SME collaboration: Interview scheduling, Review requests, Draft feedback — all tracked
- AI content assist: Generate drafts from briefs and SME interviews, Suggest angles, Create variations for channels
- Review workflow: Draft → SME review → Marketing polish → Legal (if needed) → Approved
- Repurposing tracker: From each hero piece, Track derivative content (Blog → LinkedIn → Email → Slides)
- Performance tracking: Views, engagement, leads generated by content
- Dashboard: Content published this month, Pipeline, SME participation, Performance by type

Include automations:
- When content planned, schedule SME interview
- When draft complete, route to SME for review
- When published, AI generates social posts for review
- Monthly content performance report
- Quarterly content audit: What to refresh, What to retire
```

**Outcome**: 
- Consistent thought leadership output
- Efficient SME involvement (respect their time)
- Every hero piece generates multiple derivative pieces
- Clear visibility into what's working

**Discovery Questions**:
- "How much thought leadership content do you publish? How much would you like to publish?"
- "What's the biggest bottleneck — getting SME time, or producing the content?"
- "How do you repurpose content across channels?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
IT services buyers value expertise over promotion. Technical credibility matters. Gartner and Forrester coverage is valuable. Long-form content (whitepapers, research) establishes authority.

### 2. Case Study & Reference Management
**Relevance**: High  
**Value Driver**: 3 (Scale Without Overhead)

**Pain Point**: 
Case studies are essential for services marketing — they prove capability. But producing them is painful: client approval is slow, results data isn't captured, and the same stories are recreated for every sales situation. Many successful projects never become case studies.

**Solution**: 
monday.com case study pipeline that systematically captures wins. Track project outcomes, manage client approval, and maintain a searchable library that sales can self-serve.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a Case Study & Reference Management app for IT services marketing teams.

Purpose: Systematically produce case studies and manage client references for sales support.

Key features:
- Case study candidates: Identify successful projects, Potential case study rating, Client relationship strength
- Outcome capture: Business results, Technical details, Client quotes — captured from projects
- Client approval workflow: Request → Follow-up → Approved/Declined, Approval level (full/limited/anonymous)
- Production tracking: Writer assigned, Draft date, Review cycles, Publish date
- Asset library: Written case study, Video testimonial, Slides, Quote cards
- Taxonomy: Industry, Service type, Technology, Challenge type — for easy search
- Reference program: Referenceable clients, Approved for calls/visits, Last used
- Dashboard: Case studies by status, By industry, By service, References available, Approval pending

Include automations:
- When project closes successfully, prompt PM for case study consideration
- When client approval requested, track follow-up sequence
- When case study published, notify sales and distribute
- Quarterly case study pipeline review
- When reference used, track and log
```

**Outcome**: 
- More case studies from successful projects
- Faster case study production
- Searchable library for sales
- Active reference program

**Discovery Questions**:
- "What percentage of successful projects become case studies?"
- "How quickly can sales find a relevant case study?"
- "How do you manage client references?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
B2B IT services buyers rely heavily on references and case studies. Client approval is often the bottleneck. Quantified results (cost savings, time reduction) are most valuable. Industry-specific proof points matter.

### 3. Demand Generation & Lead Management
**Relevance**: High  
**Value Driver**: 2 (Consolidate Tech Stack)

**Pain Point**: 
Services marketing must generate qualified leads for long-cycle sales. But lead quality is inconsistent, attribution is unclear, and the handoff to sales is messy. Marketing is measured on lead quantity; sales complains about lead quality.

**Solution**: 
monday.com demand generation and lead management connecting campaigns to qualified opportunities. Track leads from source through qualification, score based on fit and engagement, and hand off to sales with full context.

**Vibe Prompt**:
```
Build a Demand Generation & Lead Management app for IT services marketing teams.

Purpose: Generate, score, and qualify leads with clear attribution and sales handoff.

Key features:
- Lead capture: Source (Content/Event/Webinar/Referral/Website/Paid), Contact info, Company, Role
- Lead scoring: Fit score (company size, industry, role) + Engagement score (content consumed, events attended)
- Lead enrichment: AI enrichment with company and contact data
- Qualification workflow: MQL → Sales accepts/rejects → SQL → Opportunity
- Campaign tracking: Which campaigns generated which leads? ROI by campaign
- Nurture sequences: For leads not ready, Track nurture content consumed
- Sales handoff: Context package (source, content consumed, engagement history)
- Attribution: Which touchpoints influenced conversion?
- Dashboard: Leads by stage, By source, Conversion rates, Campaign ROI, Sales acceptance rate

Include automations:
- When lead captured, score and route based on score
- When lead score reaches threshold, notify sales for qualification
- When sales accepts lead, create opportunity
- When lead rejected, capture reason and route to nurture
- Monthly demand gen performance report
```

**Outcome**: 
- Better lead quality through scoring
- Clear attribution for ROI
- Smoother sales handoff
- Marketing-sales alignment

**Discovery Questions**:
- "How do you score and qualify leads?"
- "What's your marketing-to-sales qualified lead conversion rate?"
- "Can you attribute revenue to specific marketing activities?"

**Industry-Specific Context**: 
IT services sales cycles are long (3-12 months). Leads need nurturing over time. "MQL" = Marketing Qualified Lead; "SQL" = Sales Qualified Lead. Fit (right company/role) matters as much as interest.

## Industry Terminology Glossary

**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)** — Lead that meets marketing criteria for quality and engagement.

**SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)** — Lead that sales has accepted as worth pursuing.

**Lead Scoring** — Ranking leads based on fit and engagement signals.

**Attribution** — Connecting marketing activities to revenue outcomes.

**Thought Leadership** — Content demonstrating expertise; positions firm as authority.

**Analyst Relations** — Managing relationships with industry analysts (Gartner, Forrester, IDC).

**Magic Quadrant** — Gartner's positioning research; highly influential for IT services.

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — Definition of target customer by industry, size, role, etc.

**ABM (Account-Based Marketing)** — Targeting specific companies with personalized marketing.

**Nurture** — Marketing to leads over time until they're ready to buy.
