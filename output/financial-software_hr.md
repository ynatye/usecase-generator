# Financial Software × HR
## monday.com SE Playbook

### Industry Context

The financial software industry operates in one of the most regulated and rapidly evolving technology sectors, where HR departments face unprecedented challenges in scaling talent while maintaining strict compliance standards. Fintech companies must navigate complex regulatory frameworks including FINRA registrations, SOX compliance, and various financial services licensing requirements while competing for scarce engineering talent with deep financial domain expertise. These organizations often experience explosive growth phases, scaling from 50 to 500+ employees in months, requiring HR systems that can handle rapid onboarding, equity compensation complexity, and distributed team management across multiple time zones.

HR teams in financial software companies must balance aggressive hiring targets with rigorous background checks, manage sophisticated equity compensation packages including RSUs and stock options with cliff vesting, and ensure continuous compliance training for evolving financial regulations. The pressure is amplified by high-stress environments where burnout is common, M&A activity requiring cultural integration, and the need to maintain employer branding that attracts top-tier engineering talent while meeting strict regulatory and audit requirements.

### Department Profile
- **Typical Team Size:** 3-15 (scaling rapidly), with dedicated compliance and talent acquisition specialists
- **Key Stakeholders:** Chief People Officer, Head of Talent Acquisition, Compliance Officers, Engineering Leadership, Legal/Risk teams
- **Core KPIs:** Time-to-hire for engineering roles, regulatory compliance scores, employee retention rates, equity administration accuracy, background check processing time
- **Common Tools Replaced:** Workday, BambooHR, Lever, Greenhouse, Carta, Global Relay, various point solutions for compliance tracking
