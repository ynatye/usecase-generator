## Industry Terminology

**Co-employment:** Legal doctrine where both staffing firm and client company may be considered joint employers with shared liability

**Master Service Agreement (MSA):** Umbrella contract governing the overall relationship between staffing firm and client

**Statement of Work (SOW):** Project-specific work order under an MSA defining scope, rates, and terms

**Worker Misclassification:** Incorrect designation of workers as independent contractors rather than employees

**Joint Employer:** Two or more entities that exercise control over essential terms and conditions of employment

**Economic Realities Test:** DOL test examining the economic relationship to determine employee vs. contractor status

**Right-to-Control Test:** Common law test focusing on the employer's right to control how work is performed

**Triangular Employment Relationship:** Complex employment relationship involving staffing firm, client company, and temporary worker

**Employment Eligibility Verification (I-9):** Federal form documenting employee authorization to work in the United States

**E-Verify:** Electronic system confirming employment eligibility of workers
