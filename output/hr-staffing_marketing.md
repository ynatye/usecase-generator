## Industry Terminology

### Key Terms
- **ATS (Applicant Tracking System)** - Software for managing candidate applications and recruitment workflows
- **VMS (Vendor Management System)** - Platform used by clients to manage staffing suppliers and contractors
- **MSP (Managed Service Provider)** - Staffing firm that manages all contingent workforce needs for a client
- **RPO (Recruitment Process Outsourcing)** - Complete outsourcing of recruitment functions to a staffing partner
- **Contingent Workforce** - Temporary, contract, and freelance workers
- **Direct Hire/Permanent Placement** - Full-time employee placements with clients
- **Contract-to-Hire** - Temporary assignments with potential for permanent conversion
- **Fill Rate** - Percentage of job orders successfully filled with candidates
- **Time-to-Fill** - Days from job order receipt to candidate start date
- **Markup/Margin** - Difference between bill rate (charged to client) and pay rate (paid to candidate)
- **Submittal-to-Interview Ratio** - Percentage of candidate submissions that result in client interviews
- **Candidate Quality Score** - Internal rating system for evaluating candidate fit and likelihood of success

### Marketing-Specific Terms
- **Cost-per-Hire** - Total marketing and recruitment costs divided by number of placements
- **Cost-per-Click (CPC)** - Amount paid for each click on job advertisements
- **Cost-per-Application** - Marketing spend divided by number of completed applications
- **Application Completion Rate** - Percentage of started applications that are completed
- **Candidate Conversion Funnel** - Tracking candidates from initial contact through placement
- **Source of Hire** - Channel through which successful candidates were originally identified
- **Employer Brand Score** - Measure of company reputation and attractiveness to candidates
- **Passive Candidate** - Employed individual not actively seeking new opportunities
- **Active Candidate** - Individual actively searching for new employment
- **Silver Medalist Program** - Strategy for nurturing second-choice candidates for future opportunities

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors
- **Large National Staffing Firms** (Robert Half, Adecco, Randstad, ManpowerGroup)
  - *Advantages:* Brand recognition, massive candidate databases, extensive geographic coverage
  - *Disadvantages:* Less personalized service, slower decision-making, higher costs
  - *Marketing Approach:* Large advertising budgets, broad-based campaigns, national job board dominance

- **Mid-Market Regional Players** (Local and regional staffing companies)
  - *Advantages:* Local market knowledge, personal relationships, specialized industry focus
  - *Disadvantages:* Limited resources, smaller candidate pools, technology gaps
  - *Marketing Approach:* Relationship-based marketing, local event participation, niche specialization

- **Boutique/Specialized Firms** (Executive search, niche industry specialists)
  - *Advantages:* Deep industry expertise, high-touch service, premium positioning
  - *Disadvantages:* Limited scalability, narrow market focus, dependency on key relationships
  - *Marketing Approach:* Thought leadership, industry conference presence, referral programs

### Technology Disruption
- **AI-Powered Platforms** (HiringSolved, Eightfold, Pymetrics)
  - *Threat:* Direct client access to candidate sourcing and matching technology
  - *Response:* Emphasize human judgment, relationship value, and service quality

- **Gig Economy Platforms** (Upwork, Freelancer, Toptal)
  - *Threat:* Direct contractor-client connections, lower costs, faster turnaround
  - *Response:* Focus on quality assurance, risk management, and ongoing support

- **Internal Corporate Recruiting** (Enhanced in-house capabilities)
  - *Threat:* Clients bringing more recruiting functions internal
  - *Response:* Position as strategic partner for specialized needs and overflow capacity
