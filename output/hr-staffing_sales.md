## Industry Terminology

**Client-Side Terms:**
- **MSP (Managed Service Provider)**: Large-scale staffing program management
- **RPO (Recruitment Process Outsourcing)**: Complete recruitment function outsourcing
- **SOW (Statement of Work)**: Project-based staffing agreements
- **MSA (Master Service Agreement)**: Umbrella contract for ongoing services
- **VMS (Vendor Management System)**: Technology platform for managing multiple staffing vendors
- **Direct Hire**: Permanent placement recruiting
- **Contract-to-Hire**: Temporary placement with conversion option
- **Payroll Services**: Employer of record for contractor payments

**Sales-Specific Terms:**
- **Bill Rate**: Hourly rate charged to client
- **Pay Rate**: Hourly rate paid to contractor
- **Markup/Margin**: Difference between bill and pay rates
- **Submittal**: Candidate presentation to client
- **Right to Represent**: Exclusive candidate representation agreement
- **Rebate/Guarantee**: Refund provision for early terminations
- **Fill Rate**: Percentage of job orders successfully completed
- **Time to Fill**: Average days from job order to placement

**Operational Terms:**
- **Bench**: Available contractors between assignments
- **Backfill**: Replacement for terminated contractor
- **Extension**: Contract duration increase
- **Conversion**: Contractor becoming permanent employee
- **Onboarding**: New hire integration process
- **Offboarding**: Contract completion process

## Objection Handling

### "We already have a staffing vendor relationship"
**Response**: "That's great - successful companies often work with multiple partners to ensure coverage and competitive performance. Our clients typically see 23% better fill rates and 31% faster time-to-hire when they have a strategic secondary partner. What gaps or challenges are you experiencing with your current provider that we might address?"

### "Your rates are higher than our current vendor"
**Response**: "I appreciate the direct feedback on pricing. Our clients typically see total cost of ownership improvements despite higher bill rates due to better retention (average 34% longer assignments), faster fills (reducing opportunity costs), and higher-quality placements. What's the real cost impact when positions remain unfilled for weeks longer?"

### "We're not looking to change vendors right now"
**Response**: "I understand - changing established relationships isn't something companies take lightly. Most of our best clients weren't looking to change when we first met either. However, market conditions are evolving rapidly. What if we could demonstrate capabilities that could enhance your current program without disrupting existing relationships?"

### "We handle recruiting internally"
**Response**: "That's a strategic advantage - internal teams bring institutional knowledge that external partners can't match. Even our most successful clients with strong internal capabilities use us for overflow capacity, specialized skill sets, or geographic expansion. What recruiting challenges keep you up at night that additional capacity might solve?"

### "Staffing is a commodity - price is the only differentiator"
**Response**: "I hear that perspective often, and frankly, some vendors have commoditized themselves through lack of differentiation. However, our clients see measurable differences in quality, speed, and retention that directly impact their business outcomes. What would change in your business if you could consistently fill critical roles 40% faster with candidates who stay 50% longer?"

### "We're reducing our contingent workforce spend"
**Response**: "That's a smart strategic move given economic uncertainties. Interestingly, our most successful partnerships have developed during similar consolidation periods. Companies that work with fewer, more strategic partners often see better results at lower total costs. How are you measuring success in your workforce strategy beyond just cost reduction?"

### "We need to see references from similar companies"
**Response**: "Absolutely - references are critical for any strategic decision. I'd be happy to connect you with similar clients, but first let me understand your specific requirements so I can match you with the most relevant references. What particular challenges or outcomes would you want to discuss with our existing clients?"

### "Your company is too small to handle our volume"
**Response**: "Size is definitely an important consideration for scalability. However, our clients often find that mid-sized partners provide more agility, attention, and innovation than larger competitors. We've successfully scaled several clients from startup to enterprise levels. What specific volume requirements and scalability concerns should we address?"
