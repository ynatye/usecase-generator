## Use Case #1: Crisis Communications Response Management

### Pain Point
When scandals, fund misuse allegations, or operational crises hit, non-profits have 24-48 hours to respond before narrative damage becomes permanent. Current process involves frantic email chains, lost context, unclear approval workflows, and inconsistent messaging across channels.

### Solution
monday.com Work Management + CRM + AI Agents create a crisis response war room:
- **Automated Crisis Detection**: AI agents monitor news, social media, and regulatory filings for mentions
- **Response Workflow**: Pre-built templates trigger stakeholder notification sequences
- **Approval Matrix**: Board members, legal, and comms teams collaborate in real-time
- **Multi-channel Publishing**: Coordinated responses across website, social, email, and media

### Outcome
- Crisis response time reduced from 48+ hours to 4-6 hours
- 90% reduction in conflicting public statements
- Legal review integrated into workflow, reducing compliance risk
- Stakeholder confidence maintained through transparent, timely communication

### Discovery Questions
- "Walk me through your last crisis - how did information flow between board, legal, and comms?"
- "How do you currently monitor for potential reputation threats?"
- "What's your biggest fear when a negative story breaks about your organization?"
- "Who needs to approve statements before they go public, and how long does that take?"

### Industry Context
Non-profits face unique reputational vulnerabilities - donor trust is fragile, regulatory scrutiny is high, and mission credibility directly impacts funding. Unlike corporations, they can't "buy" their way out of reputation damage.

### Vibe Prompt
*"You're the Communications Director getting a 6am call about a front-page exposé. Your board is panicking, donors are calling, and social media is exploding. You need to respond perfectly, quickly, and with everyone aligned. Show me how monday.com becomes your crisis command center."*

### Agent Blueprint
**Crisis Response AI Agent**:
- **Triggers**: Negative sentiment spikes, regulatory filings, board member mentions
- **Actions**: Alert stakeholder groups, compile relevant background docs, suggest response frameworks
- **Integrations**: Google Alerts, social listening tools, legal document repositories
- **Outputs**: Situation briefs, response templates, stakeholder contact sequences

## Industry Terminology

**Earned Media**: Coverage obtained through PR efforts rather than paid advertising
**Stakeholder Stewardship**: Ongoing relationship management with key supporters and partners
**Coalition Building**: Process of forming strategic partnerships with aligned organizations
**Issue Advocacy**: Communications focused on specific policy or social issues rather than the organization
**Crisis Messaging**: Strategic communications during reputation-threatening situations
**Donor Journey**: The progression of donor relationship from awareness to major giving
**Program Impact**: Measurable outcomes of non-profit programs and services
**Cause Marketing**: Marketing that promotes both social causes and organizational mission
**Grant Narrative**: Storytelling approach used in funding proposals and reports
**Beneficiary Voice**: Authentic representation of those served by the organization
**Social License**: Public approval and credibility necessary for organizational effectiveness
**Mission Alignment**: Ensuring all communications reinforce core organizational purpose
