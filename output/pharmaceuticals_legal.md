## Industry Terminology

**ANDA (Abbreviated New Drug Application)**: Generic drug application filed with FDA, often triggering Paragraph IV patent challenges

**Paragraph IV Certification**: Generic company's assertion that branded patents are invalid or will not be infringed, triggering automatic 30-month stay

**Hatch-Waxman Act**: Law governing generic drug approval and patent litigation, including 180-day exclusivity provisions

**BPCIA (Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act)**: Biosimilar approval pathway including complex "patent dance" procedures

**Patent Dance**: BPCIA-mandated information exchange between biosimilar and reference product sponsors regarding patent disputes

**Form 483**: FDA inspection observation document requiring formal company response and corrective action

**Sunshine Act**: Federal law requiring pharmaceutical companies to report payments to healthcare providers

**Anti-Kickback Statute**: Federal law prohibiting improper payments to healthcare providers to induce business

**FCPA (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act)**: Federal anti-corruption law governing international business operations

**GCP (Good Clinical Practice)**: International quality standards for clinical trial conduct and data integrity

**CTA (Clinical Trial Agreement)**: Contract governing relationships between sponsors and clinical investigators

**Pharmacovigilance**: Post-market safety surveillance and adverse event reporting requirements

**Orange Book**: FDA publication listing approved drugs and patent information for generic drug applications

**Purple Book**: FDA database of licensed biological products and patent information for biosimilars

**505(b)(2) Application**: Hybrid FDA approval pathway relying partly on studies not conducted by applicant
