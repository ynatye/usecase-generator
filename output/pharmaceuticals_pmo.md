## Industry Terminology

### Clinical Development
- **Protocol**: Detailed study plan outlining objectives, methodology, and procedures
- **CDISC**: Clinical Data Interchange Standards Consortium - data standards for clinical trials
- **eCTD**: Electronic Common Technical Document - standard format for regulatory submissions
- **ALCOA**: Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate - data integrity principles
- **GCP**: Good Clinical Practice - international quality standards for clinical trials
- **CSR**: Clinical Study Report - comprehensive document summarizing clinical trial results

### Regulatory Affairs
- **BLA**: Biologics License Application - FDA application for biological products
- **NDA**: New Drug Application - FDA application for new pharmaceuticals
- **MAA**: Marketing Authorization Application - European equivalent of NDA
- **CRL**: Complete Response Letter - FDA rejection with requirements for resubmission
- **PDUFA**: Prescription Drug User Fee Act - defines FDA review timelines
- **RTF**: Refuse to File - FDA refusal to review submission due to deficiencies

### Manufacturing & Quality
- **cGMP**: Current Good Manufacturing Practices - FDA quality requirements
- **PAT**: Process Analytical Technology - real-time manufacturing monitoring
- **OOS**: Out of Specification - test results outside acceptable limits
- **CAPA**: Corrective and Preventive Actions - quality system improvement process
- **API**: Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient - the drug substance in medications
- **QbD**: Quality by Design - systematic approach to pharmaceutical development

### Commercial & Market Access
- **HEOR**: Health Economics and Outcomes Research - evidence of drug value
- **RWE**: Real-World Evidence - data from actual clinical use
- **HTA**: Health Technology Assessment - evaluation by payer organizations
- **ICER**: Institute for Clinical and Economic Review - US cost-effectiveness evaluator
- **P&T**: Pharmacy and Therapeutics Committee - hospital formulary decision-makers
- **Prior Authorization**: Payer requirement for approval before prescribing
