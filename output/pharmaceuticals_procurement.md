The pharmaceutical procurement landscape is uniquely complex, demanding stringent regulatory compliance, supply chain resilience, and cost optimization while maintaining patient safety standards. **Core Value Drivers:**

## Industry Terminology

### Regulatory & Compliance
- **API (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient)** - The biologically active component of a drug product
- **GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice)** - Quality standards for pharmaceutical manufacturing
- **CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)** - Quality management process for addressing non-conformances
- **Form 483** - FDA document issued to company management at the conclusion of an inspection
- **ICH (International Council for Harmonisation)** - Standards for pharmaceutical development and registration
- **GDP (Good Distribution Practice)** - Quality standards for pharmaceutical distribution
- **Pharmacovigilance** - Science and activities relating to detection and prevention of adverse drug effects

### Supply Chain & Procurement
- **CRO (Contract Research Organization)** - Company providing research services to pharmaceutical industry
- **CMO (Contract Manufacturing Organization)** - Company providing manufacturing services
- **CDMO (Contract Development and Manufacturing Organization)** - Combined development and manufacturing services
- **Single-source** - One supplier chosen from multiple options
- **Sole-source** - Only one supplier available globally
- **Excipient** - Inactive substance serving as vehicle or medium for active ingredient
- **Maverick spend** - Procurement outside of established processes and contracts

### Quality & Manufacturing
- **QbD (Quality by Design)** - Systematic approach to development beginning with predefined objectives
- **PAT (Process Analytical Technology)** - Framework for designing and controlling manufacturing
- **Cold Chain** - Temperature-controlled supply chain for temperature-sensitive products
- **Serialization** - Process of applying unique identifiers to pharmaceutical packages
- **Aggregation** - Process of recording relationships between different packaging levels
