## Industry Terminology

### Legal Technology Terms
- **Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM)**: End-to-end contract management from drafting through renewal
- **Legal Operations (LegalOps)**: Business discipline focused on optimizing legal team efficiency and effectiveness
- **Matter Management**: Systematic approach to tracking and managing legal matters and cases
- **Legal Spend Management**: Process of controlling and optimizing legal expenses and external counsel costs
- **Regulatory Technology (RegTech)**: Technology solutions designed to address regulatory challenges efficiently

### R&D Legal Specific Terms
- **Technology Transfer Office (TTO)**: University department managing commercialization of research innovations
- **Invention Disclosure**: Formal documentation of potentially patentable research outcomes
- **Sponsored Research Agreement (SRA)**: Contract defining terms for externally funded research projects
- **Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA)**: Partnership between federal laboratories and private entities
- **Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR)**: Federal program funding early-stage research commercialization

### Compliance & Risk Terms
- **Export Administration Regulations (EAR)**: US regulations controlling export of dual-use items and technologies
- **International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR)**: US regulations controlling defense-related articles and services
- **Institutional Review Board (IRB)**: Committee reviewing research involving human subjects for ethical compliance
- **Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)**: Quality standards for pharmaceutical and medical device production
- **Research Integrity**: Adherence to ethical principles and professional standards in research conduct

### Intellectual Property Terms
- **Freedom to Operate (FTO)**: Analysis ensuring products/services don't infringe existing patents
- **Prior Art Search**: Investigation of existing knowledge to assess patentability of inventions
- **Patent Landscape Analysis**: Comprehensive review of patents in specific technology area
- **Defensive Patent Strategy**: Building patent portfolio to protect against litigation rather than generate revenue
- **Patent Thicket**: Dense web of overlapping patents that may impede innovation
