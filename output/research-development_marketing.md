## Use Case #1: Research Commercialization Pipeline Management

### Pain
R&D organizations struggle to track and market their research from lab bench to market launch. ### Solution
monday.com Work Management + AI Agents + mondayDB creates an intelligent research commercialization pipeline that automatically tracks projects through TRL (Technology Readiness Levels), manages IP protection status, identifies commercialization opportunities, and generates targeted marketing campaigns for each technology asset.

### Outcome
- 75% reduction in time spent on project status tracking and reporting
- 3x faster time-to-market for commercializable research
- 40% increase in successful technology transfer agreements
- Replace 2-3 FTE project coordinators with AI-driven automation

## Industry Terminology

**Technology Readiness Levels (TRL)**: 1-9 scale measuring technology maturation from basic research (TRL 1) to commercial deployment (TRL 9)

**Technology Transfer Office (TTO)**: University/research institution office responsible for commercializing research through patents, licensing, and spin-offs

**SBIR/STTR**: Small Business Innovation Research/Small Business Technology Transfer programs providing R&D funding for small businesses

**Intellectual Property (IP) Portfolio**: Collection of patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets owned by an organization

**Commercialization Pipeline**: Systematic process for moving research discoveries from lab to market

**Principal Investigator (PI)**: Lead researcher responsible for research project direction and outcomes

**Industry-Sponsored Research Agreement (ISRA)**: Contract between research organization and industry partner for collaborative R&D

**Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA)**: Legal agreement enabling collaboration between federal labs and private industry

**Research Impact Factor**: Metric measuring citation frequency and academic influence of research publications

**Proof of Concept (POC)**: Demonstration that research findings can be developed into viable commercial applications
