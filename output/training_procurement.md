## Use Case 1: LMS & Content Platform Vendor Selection

### Pain Point
Training companies spend 6-12 months evaluating Learning Management Systems and content platforms, involving multiple stakeholders across IT, curriculum, finance, and operations. The process involves complex RFPs, vendor demos, technical evaluations, and compliance reviews that often derail due to poor coordination and lost information.

### Solution
**monday.com Work Management + AI Agents + Vibe**
- Centralized vendor evaluation workspace with automated RFP distribution
- AI agents parse vendor responses and create standardized comparison matrices
- Vibe handles initial vendor qualification conversations
- Automated compliance and security requirement tracking
- Real-time scoring dashboards for stakeholder input

### Outcome
- 70% reduction in vendor selection timeline (12 months → 3.5 months)
- 85% improvement in vendor response completeness
- Eliminate 40 hours/week of manual data consolidation
- Replace need for dedicated procurement coordinator role

### Discovery Questions
1. "How many LMS vendors did you evaluate in your last selection process, and how long did it take from RFP to contract?"
2. "Who are the key stakeholders involved in your platform decisions, and how do you currently coordinate their feedback?"
3. "What's your biggest frustration with vendor evaluation processes?"
4. "How do you currently track technical requirements and compliance standards?"
5. "What happens when a vendor demo doesn't align with your original RFP requirements?"

### Industry Context
Training companies typically evaluate 15-30 LMS vendors, with enterprise deals ranging from $50K-$500K annually. The process involves curriculum teams (learning objectives), IT teams (technical requirements), compliance teams (data protection), and finance teams (cost modeling). Failed implementations cost an average of $250K in lost time and resources.

### Vibe Prompt
*"You're a procurement specialist for learning organizations. You understand the complexity of evaluating educational technology platforms, the importance of learner experience, and the need to balance functionality with cost. You speak the language of instructional design, technical specifications, and vendor management. You help training companies make confident technology decisions that impact thousands of learners."*

### Agent Blueprint
**Vendor Evaluation Agent**
- **Triggers:** New vendor inquiry, RFP release, demo scheduled

- **Data Sources:** mondayDB vendor profiles, requirement templates, scoring frameworks

## Industry Terminology

**Key Terms Training Procurement Professionals Use:**

- **SCORM/xAPI/cmi5:** eLearning technical standards for content interoperability
- **LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability):** Standard for integrating learning tools with platforms
- **AICC:** Aviation Industry CBT Committee standards for learning content
- **SME (Subject Matter Expert):** Domain experts who develop or review training content
- **ISD (Instructional Systems Design):** Systematic approach to training development
- **Kirkpatrick Model:** Four-level framework for evaluating training effectiveness
- **ADDIE:** Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation methodology
- **Microlearning:** Short-form, focused learning modules
- **Gamification:** Application of game elements to learning experiences
- **Adaptive Learning:** Technology that adjusts content based on learner performance
- **Proctoring Services:** Remote supervision for online examinations
- **Accessibility Compliance:** WCAG 2.1 AA standards for learner accommodation
- **White-label Solutions:** Customizable platforms that can be branded for clients
- **API Integration:** Technical capability for connecting learning platforms
- **Single Sign-On (SSO):** Authentication system for seamless platform access
