## Prioritized Use Cases

### 1. Brand Campaign Management Hub
**Relevance**: 🔴 HIGH  
**Value Driver**: Consolidate Tech Stack with AI

**Pain Point**: With 50+ global brands and the Kellanova integration, Mars Snacking runs hundreds of campaigns simultaneously across markets. Campaign information is scattered across email, spreadsheets, agency portals, and legacy tools from both Mars and Kellanova.

**Solution**: monday.com as the single source of truth for all campaign planning and execution:
- Campaign briefs, timelines, and assets in one place
- Real-time status visibility across brands and regions
- AI-powered campaign health monitoring and risk alerts
- Integration with creative asset management (DAM)

**Outcome**: 
- 40% reduction in campaign coordination time
- Single view of all active campaigns globally
- Faster identification of at-risk campaigns

**Discovery Questions**:
1. "How do you currently track campaign status across your brand portfolio — and has Kellanova integration complicated that?"
2. "When a campaign is at risk of missing a launch date, how quickly does leadership know?"
3. "How many tools does your marketing team touch in a typical campaign workflow?"
4. "What percentage of your team's time goes to status updates vs. strategic work?"

**Mars-Specific Context**: Post-Kellanova, Mars Snacking now manages legacy Kellogg's brands alongside Mars/Wrigley brands. Two different campaign management cultures and tech stacks need unification.

### 2. Marketing Asset & Creative Operations
**Relevance**: 🔴 HIGH  
**Value Driver**: Scale Impact Without Overhead

**Pain Point**: Managing creative assets for 50+ brands across 145 markets creates massive operational overhead. Assets get lost, versioning is chaotic, approvals bottleneck, and agencies work in silos.

**Solution**: Centralized creative operations workflow:
- Structured intake forms for creative briefs
- Automated routing to internal teams or agencies
- Version control and approval workflows
- AI-powered asset tagging and search
- Regional adaptation tracking

**Outcome**:
- 50% faster creative turnaround
- Elimination of "lost asset" searches
- Clear audit trail for compliance

**Discovery Questions**:
1. "How do you manage creative requests across your brand portfolio today?"
2. "When a regional team needs to adapt a global asset, what does that process look like?"
3. "How often do you struggle to find the latest approved version of an asset?"
4. "What's your current approval workflow for creative, and where does it break down?"

**Mars-Specific Context**: M&M's alone has massive creative needs (characters, seasonal variants, collaborations). Hotel Chocolat has distinct premium brand guidelines. Kellanova brands have their own visual systems. Unifying creative ops is critical.

### 3. Integrated Marketing Calendar
**Relevance**: 🔴 HIGH  
**Value Driver**: Consolidate Tech Stack with AI

**Pain Point**: Marketing calendars exist in silos — by brand, by region, by channel. No one has a holistic view of what's launching when, leading to internal competition for shelf space, overlapping promotions, and missed synergies.

**Solution**: Global marketing calendar with:
- Multi-brand, multi-market visibility
- Retail partner promotional windows (Walmart, Target, Tesco, etc.)
- Seasonal and tentpole event planning (Halloween, Easter, Super Bowl)
- AI-powered conflict detection and optimization suggestions
- Integration with trade marketing and retail execution

**Outcome**:
- Coordinated promotional calendar across portfolio
- Reduced internal brand cannibalization
- Better leverage of tentpole moments

**Discovery Questions**:
1. "How do you coordinate marketing calendars across brands to avoid stepping on each other?"
2. "For a major retail partner like Walmart, how do you manage promotional windows across your portfolio?"
3. "How do you plan for tentpole moments like Halloween or Super Bowl across all your brands?"
4. "Post-Kellanova, how are you integrating their promotional calendar with legacy Mars planning?"

**Mars-Specific Context**: Halloween is massive for Mars (Snickers, M&M's, Skittles). Kellanova owns breakfast (Pop-Tarts, Eggo). Easter, Back-to-School, and Super Bowl are all portfolio-wide opportunities requiring coordination.

## Industry Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CPG** | Consumer Packaged Goods — industry category for Mars Snacking products |
| **FMCG** | Fast-Moving Consumer Goods — alternative term, more common outside US |
| **Trade Marketing** | Marketing focused on retail partners (Walmart, Target) vs. consumers |
| **Shopper Marketing** | Marketing at point of purchase to influence buying decisions |
| **Planogram** | Visual diagram of product placement on retail shelves |
| **Tentpole** | Major seasonal or cultural event (Super Bowl, Halloween) used for marketing |
| **DAM** | Digital Asset Management — system for storing and organizing creative assets |
| **SOW** | Statement of Work — contract defining agency deliverables |
| **IMC** | Integrated Marketing Communications — coordinated multi-channel campaigns |
| **Brand Architecture** | How brands in a portfolio relate to each other |
| **Cannibalization** | When brands in same portfolio compete for same customers |
| **Share of Voice** | Brand's advertising presence relative to competitors |
