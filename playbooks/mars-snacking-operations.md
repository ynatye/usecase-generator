## Prioritized Use Cases

### 1. Post-Acquisition Integration Command Center
**Relevance**: 🔴 CRITICAL  
**Value Driver**: Consolidate Tech Stack with AI

**Pain Point**: The $36B Kellanova acquisition (closed Dec 2025) is the largest operational integration in Mars history. Two massive organizations with different:
- Manufacturing footprints
- Supply chain networks
- ERP systems (likely SAP vs Oracle)
- Operating procedures and cultures

Integration projects are tracked in spreadsheets, emails, and disconnected project tools. Leadership lacks real-time visibility into integration progress and risks.

**Solution**: Unified integration management hub:
- All integration workstreams in one place (supply chain, manufacturing, IT, HR, etc.)
- Real-time milestone tracking with dependencies
- Risk and issue management with escalation workflows
- AI-powered status summarization for leadership
- Cross-functional coordination across Mars and legacy Kellanova teams

**Outcome**:
- Accelerated integration timeline (months saved)
- Early identification of integration risks
- Single source of truth for leadership reporting
- Reduced integration cost through better coordination

**Discovery Questions**:
1. "What's your current approach to tracking Kellanova integration across all workstreams?"
2. "How does leadership get visibility into integration progress and risks?"
3. "What's the biggest coordination challenge between legacy Mars and Kellanova teams?"
4. "How many tools and spreadsheets are involved in integration tracking today?"
5. "Have you identified any integration risks that weren't caught early enough?"

**Mars-Specific Context**: This is the biggest CPG acquisition since Kraft-Heinz. Integration success will define Mars Snacking's operational efficiency for the next decade. Stakes are enormous.

### 3. Supply Chain Risk & Resilience Management
**Relevance**: 🔴 HIGH

**Outcome**:

- Reduced disruption impact through faster response

- Better supplier relationship management

**Discovery Questions**:

- Cross-functional visibility that ERPs don't provide
- Workflow flexibility that rigid systems lack
- Human-in-the-loop coordination for exception management

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We have SAP for this" | "SAP is your system of record — we're not replacing it. monday.com is the coordination layer for the work that happens between systems. The cross-functional visibility, exception management, and human workflows that SAP wasn't designed for." |
| "We're in the middle of Kellanova integration — no bandwidth" | "The integration IS the use case. You're trying to coordinate two organizations with different systems. monday.com can be the neutral ground where both teams collaborate, without waiting for ERP consolidation." |
| "Operations doesn't use monday.com — it's too 'soft'" | "Let me show you how other manufacturing operations use it — not for production scheduling (that's MES) but for the coordination, projects, and cross-functional work that falls between systems. The stuff that lives in email and Excel today." |
| "We need deep manufacturing functionality" | "Agreed — and you have that in your MES and ERP. What's missing is the flexible workflow layer for everything else: quality investigations, capital projects, supplier issues, integration workstreams. That's monday.com's sweet spot." |
| "IT won't approve another tool" | "Let's position this as reducing tool sprawl, not adding to it. How many spreadsheets and shadow tools exist because your core systems don't handle coordination well? monday.com consolidates that chaos." |
